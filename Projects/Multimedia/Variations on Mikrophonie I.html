Variations on Mikrophonie I

Stockhausen's Mikrophonie I is a piece composed in 1964 for a group of six players (2 trios). Each trio is comprised of one person producing sounds by striking or exciting a large tam-tam; a second one holds a microphone to capture and amplify the various sounds produced while placing and moving the microphone across the tam-tam; and a third person gathers all sounds produced and takes control of its contour by making use of preamplifiers and resonant bandpass filters.

On Variations on Mikrophonie I the visitor is invited to generate sounds from the tam-tam, by enabling the various microphones and/or using the percussive tools available, entering a world of sonic discovery. Like Stockhausen, the user unveils an otherwise hidden palette of timbres by "auscultating" the resonant sounds of the tam-tam by playing it with different objects.
