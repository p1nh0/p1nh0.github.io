HTB1 - Hypertrombone

Hypertrombone developed for the final project of the Electronic Music and Musical Production course at ESART - School of Applied Arts of the Polytechnic Institute of Castelo Branco, Portugal, 2010.

HTB1-h stands for the hardware components, comprised of an Arduino bluetooth, acceleromenter placed on the bell, force-sensing resistor on the trigger, infra-red and ultra-sound sensors to measure the silde's position and air-flow sensor made with a piezo and a small amplifier. While HTB1-s stands for the software component of the hyperinstrument. Initially it was all made with MaxMSP, while the second version read sensor data with MaxMSP translating them to MIDI, using Ableton Live for sound processing.
